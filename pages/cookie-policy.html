This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how Fluxora uses them, and what choices you have regarding their use. This policy is part of our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit them. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to provide information to the website owners.

In addition to cookies, we may use similar technologies such as:

  • Local Storage: Browser storage that persists data on your device (we use this for license activation status and usage counters).
  • Web Beacons / Pixel Tags: Small transparent images embedded in web pages or emails that help track user engagement.
  • Scripts: JavaScript code that may set or read cookie-like data during your browsing session.

Throughout this policy, we use the term "cookies" to refer to all such technologies.

4. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Service Functionality: To remember your license activation, free trial status, and usage counters — so you don't have to re-enter your license key every time you visit.
  • Analytics & Improvement: To understand which tools are most popular, how users navigate our site, and where we can improve the user experience.
  • Advertising: To display ads via Google AdSense. These ads help keep Fluxora free for users who haven't purchased a license.
  • Security: To detect and prevent fraudulent activity and protect our services from abuse.

5. Your Cookie Choices

You have several options to manage or disable cookies:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can usually:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device.
  • Delete all or specific cookies.
  • Block third-party cookies.
  • Block all cookies from specific websites.
  • Set your browser to clear cookies when you close it.

Instructions for popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Google Ad Settings

You can opt out of personalized advertising from Google by visiting:

  • Google Ads Settings — to control ads personalization across Google services.
  • Digital Advertising Alliance — to opt out of interest-based advertising from participating companies.
  • Network Advertising Initiative — another opt-out tool for interest-based advertising.

Important: Opting out of personalized ads does not mean you will stop seeing ads. You will still see ads, but they will be less relevant to your interests.

5.3 Google Analytics Opt-Out

You can prevent Google Analytics from collecting your data by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

5.4 Consequences of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable cookies, the following features may be affected:

  • License persistence: Your license activation may not be remembered between visits, requiring you to re-enter your license key.
  • Trial tracking: Your free trial status and sharing rewards may not persist.
  • Advertising: You will still see ads, but they will be less relevant.
  • Core tools: All file conversion tools will continue to work normally, as they do not require cookies.

6. Cookie Consent for EEA & UK Users

If you are visiting Fluxora from the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom, we will request your consent before placing non-essential cookies (analytics and advertising cookies) on your device, in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR).

Essential cookies (those required for basic website functionality) will be placed regardless of consent, as permitted under applicable law. You may withdraw your consent at any time by clearing your browser cookies and revisiting the website.
